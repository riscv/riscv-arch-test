[[t-M-normative_rules]]
.M Normative Rules
[cols="1,4,3", options="header"]
|===
|Normative Rule |Rule Text |Coverpoints

|mul_op |MUL performs an XLEN-bit×XLEN-bit multiplication of
rs1 by rs2 and places the lower XLEN bits in the destination
register. |M_mul_cg/{cp_rs1, cp_rs2, cp_rd, cp_rs1_rs2_edges}

|mulh_op |MULH, MULHU, and MULHSU perform the same multiplication but
return the upper XLEN bits of the full 2×XLEN-bit
product, for signed×signed,
unsigned×unsigned, and rs1×unsigned rs2 multiplication. |M_mulh_cg/{cp_rs1, cp_rs2, cp_rd, cp_rs1_rs2_edges}

|mulhu_op |see above |M_mulhu_cg/{cp_rs1, cp_rs2, cp_rd, cp_rs1_rs2_edges}

|mulhsu_op |see above |M_mulhsu_cg/{cp_rs1, cp_rs2, cp_rd, cp_rs1_rs2_edges}

|div_op |DIV and DIVU perform an XLEN bits by XLEN bits signed and unsigned
integer division of rs1 by rs2, rounding towards zero. |M_div_cg/{cp_rs1, cp_rs2, cp_rd, cp_rs1_rs2_edges}

|divu_op |see above |M_divu_op/{cp_rs1, cp_rs2, cp_rd, cp_rs1_rs2_edges}

|divw_op |DIVW and DIVUW are RV64 instructions that divide the lower 32 bits of
rs1 by the lower 32 bits of rs2, treating them as signed and
unsigned integers, placing the 32-bit quotient in rd,
sign-extended to 64 bits. |M_divw_cg/{cp_rs1, cp_rs2, cp_rd, cp_rs1_rs2_edges}

|divuw_op |see above |M_divuw_op/{cp_rs1, cp_rs2, cp_rd, cp_rs1_rs2_edges}

|rem_op |REM and REMU
provide the remainder of the corresponding division operation. |M_rem_cg/{cp_rs1, cp_rs2, cp_rd, cp_rs1_rs2_edges}

|remu_op |see above |M_remu_op/{cp_rs1, cp_rs2, cp_rd, cp_rs1_rs2_edges}

|remw_op |REMW and REMUW are RV64 instructions that
provide the corresponding signed and unsigned remainder
operations. |M_remw_cg/{cp_rs1, cp_rs2, cp_rd, cp_rs1_rs2_edges}

|remuw_op |see above |M_remuw_op/{cp_rs1, cp_rs2, cp_rd, cp_rs1_rs2_edges}

|div_by_zero |The quotient of division by zero has all bits
set |{M_div_cg, M_divu_op}/{cp_rs1_rs2_edges}

|rem_by_zero |the remainder of division by zero equals the dividend. |{M_rem_cg, M_remu_op}/{cp_rs1_rs2_edges}

|signed_div_overflow |Signed division overflow occurs only when the most-negative integer is divided
by image:../../build/images-out/stem-52c1acbf19a35be815dca1fd989d89e4.svg[width=15,height=8]. The quotient of a signed division with overflow is
equal to the dividend, and the remainder is zero. |{M_rem_cg}/{cp_rs1_rs2_edges}

|rem_result_sign |For REM,
the sign of a nonzero result equals the sign of the dividend. |{M_rem_cg}/{cp_rs1_rs2_edges}

|remw_result_sign |BothREMW and REMUW always sign-extend the 32-bit result
to 64 bits, including on a divide by zero. |{M_remw_cg/M_remuw_cg}/{cp_rs1_rs2_edges}

|remuw_result_sign |see above |{M_remw_cg/M_remuw_cg}/{cp_rs1_rs2_edges}

|mulw_op |MULW is an RV64 instruction that multiplies the lower 32 bits of the
source registers, placing the sign extension of the lower 32 bits of the
result into the destination register. |M_mulw_cg/{cp_rs1, cp_rs2, cp_rd, cp_rs1_rs2_edges}

|===
